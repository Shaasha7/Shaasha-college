National Innovation & Startup Policy

Innovation • Entrepreneurship • Startups

Shaasha College of Engineering offers higher education in Engineering, Technology and Management relevant to the current and projected needs of society. Being an autonomous institution, SCE lays a strong emphasis on collaborative research and stands apart from other institutions through its participatory work culture, student-centric activities and industry interaction. To convert knowledge into commercial products and processes, a well-defined innovation and startup policy is essential. Shaasha College of Engineering has initiated the implementation of the National Innovation and Startup Policy (NISP) proposed by the Ministry of Education Innovation Cell (MIC). This framework enables the creation of a robust innovation and startup ecosystem through Intellectual Property management, technology licensing, institutional startup policy and entrepreneurship development.

This Policy Encourages

To establish Entrepreneurship Development and Innovation Councils (EDIC) headed by qualified faculty members to guide the student-led Entrepreneurship Development Cell (EDC) to promote startups, entrepreneurship and innovation activities among students in the institution.

To permit both faculty members and students to establish startups based on research projects, innovative ideas and technology development within the institution.
